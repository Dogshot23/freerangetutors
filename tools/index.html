01

LessonTrak

LIVE FREE
v1.4

LessonTrak is a lightweight, browser-based lesson logging tool. After each session, you record what you covered, what worked, and what to come back to. There are no student profiles, no synced data, no dashboards — just a clean place to keep running notes on your teaching.

Built because keeping a proper lesson journal was always either too informal (notebook, then lost) or too heavy (spreadsheet, then abandoned). This sits in the middle — enough structure to be useful, not so much that maintaining it becomes a second job.

Per-lesson notes Works offline Saves to browser storage No account required Export as plain text Mobile-friendly

02

Report Writer

BETA FREE
v0.8 — beta

Report Writer generates varied, human-sounding student report comments from a few simple inputs. You describe the student — their level, strengths, areas to develop, and a rough tone — and the tool produces a set of draft comments you can review and adjust. Designed to handle 20 or more students without the output becoming repetitive.

Report season is genuinely exhausting. Writing 30 individual comments that sound meaningfully different from each other, that accurately reflect real students, without sounding mechanical — it's a grinding task. This doesn't replace your judgement; it removes the blank-page problem and the fatigue of repetition.

Describe level + strengths + areas to work on Generates multiple variants Avoids repetitive phrasing Tone adjustment Copy-to-clipboard output No data stored

03

ZARD

EXPERIMENTAL
v0.3 — early

ZARD — Zone-based Activity and Resource Designer — is an exploratory planning tool for thinking about the shape of a lesson. You map activities to energy zones (warm-up, focus, production, wind-down) and get a rough visual read on whether the cognitive load is distributed sensibly across the session.

There's a real difference between a lesson that's well-resourced and one that actually flows. I kept noticing that plans I thought were solid left students too flat too early, or too activated too late. ZARD is an attempt to build a thinking tool for that problem — not a lesson plan generator, more like a rough sketch you draw before writing the real thing.

Activity zone mapping Energy arc visualisation Cognitive load estimate Session arc view

This tool is at an early, exploratory stage. The concept is still being developed. Expect incomplete features, placeholder UI, and things that don't quite work yet. Shared as a public work-in-progress rather than a finished product.

04

Lesson Plan Viewer

LIVE FREE
v2.1

The Lesson Plan Viewer gives you a clean, distraction-free reading view for lesson plans during class. Paste in a plan or load a saved one, and you get a calm, readable layout — no editing mode, no menus, no clutter. Just the plan, readable at a glance from a laptop or tablet at the front of the room.

Teaching with a lesson plan document open in one tab, a timer in another, and a vocab list somewhere else is fine but fragmented. This gives one clean, scannable view for everything that matters during the lesson — with local save so you can load it back the next day without hunting for the file.

On the bench

Ideas and experiments that haven't become tools yet.

These are things being actively thought about, prototyped, or just sketched. Some will become tools. Some will get quietly abandoned. Listed here for transparency — this isn't a product roadmap.

  • WIP
    Vocabulary Gap-Fill Tool Already live in a basic form. Currently reworking the mobile layout — the current version breaks on small screens. Not updating to v2 until it works well on all devices.
  • WIP
    Lesson Timing Tool A simple session clock with labelled activity slots. Set durations for each phase, start the clock, and it tells you where you should be. First prototype is rough but usable. Needs proper mobile support before it goes on the shelf.
  • IDEA
    Error Correction Card Generator Type in student errors from a lesson, get a printable or digital set of correction prompts. The concept is clear — haven't decided yet if this is a web tool or a PDF generator.
  • IDEA
    Student Progress Snapshot A lightweight one-page summary of where a student is — built from a few quick inputs. Not a database, not a tracker. Just a snapshot to share with the student or keep for your records. Still thinking about the right format.
  • IDEA
    Lesson Pace Checker A companion to ZARD — simpler, faster. Describe the rough pace of a lesson and get a suggestion for redistribution. Might merge this into ZARD instead of making it a separate tool.

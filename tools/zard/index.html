ZARD

Zone-based Activity and Resource Designer — a planning tool for thinking about how cognitive load and energy are distributed across a lesson.

EXPERIMENTAL
v0.3 Early experiment — rough edges expected
Experimental

This tool is at an early, exploratory stage. The concept is still being developed. Expect incomplete features and things that don't quite work yet. Shared as a public work-in-progress rather than a finished product.

How to use this in class

Use ZARD during planning, not during the lesson itself. Map your intended activities to energy zones and look at the resulting arc — does it build appropriately? Does it crash too early? Does it stay flat throughout?

This is a thinking tool, not a lesson plan generator. The output is a rough visual read on whether the lesson has a sensible shape, not a prescription for how to teach it.

Most useful for lessons where something previously felt "off" in a way you couldn't pinpoint. Sometimes just the act of mapping activities to zones reveals the problem immediately.
